#  Hospital Analytics Dashboard – Quantified Report

##  Dr. Ayushi Gupta

---

##  Objective

To analyze hospital operations using data-driven insights and quantify key performance patterns related to cost, patient distribution, and treatment outcomes.

---

##  Key Metrics

* Total Patients: 200
* Departments: 6
* Doctors: 5
* Total Treatment Cost: 15.94M
* Average Recovery Score: 70.38

---

##  Quantified Analysis

### 1. Department Load

* Top 3 departments handle ~60–70% of total patients
* Remaining departments are underutilized

👉 Indicates workload imbalance across departments

---

### 2. Cost Concentration

* Top 2 departments contribute ~40–50% of total treatment cost
* Significant cost skew toward specific departments

👉 Suggests potential inefficiency or higher resource intensity

---

### 3. Treatment Cost Variation

* Maximum treatment cost: 149.34K
* Minimum treatment cost: 5.44K
* Cost variation > 25x across treatments

👉 Highlights high variability in treatment complexity

---

### 4. Cost vs Recovery Relationship

* Higher cost groups show ~15–20% improvement in recovery scores
* Indicates positive correlation between spending and outcomes

---

### 5. Length of Stay Impact

* Treatment cost increases by ~30–40% as stay duration increases
* Long stays are major cost drivers

---

##  Key Insights

* ~70% of patient load concentrated in few departments
* ~50% of cost driven by limited departments
* Treatment cost varies significantly across types
* Higher cost improves recovery but increases burden
* Length of stay strongly impacts total cost

---

##  Recommendations 

* Redistribute patient load to reduce ~20–30% pressure on high-demand departments
* Optimize high-cost departments to potentially reduce ~10–15% total expenditure
* Reduce unnecessary hospital stay to lower cost by ~15–25%
* Focus on cost-effective treatments maintaining recovery efficiency

---

##  Conclusion

The analysis shows that hospital performance is heavily influenced by department concentration, treatment cost variability, and patient stay duration. Strategic optimization in these areas can significantly improve efficiency and reduce operational costs while maintaining quality care.
